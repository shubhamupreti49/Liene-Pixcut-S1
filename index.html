Step-by-Step Guide

Follow each step in order. Tap a step to expand it.

Before anything else, verify that the machine is physically ready:

  • The machine only accepts 4×6" or 4×7" sticker/photo paper — no other sizes will work.
  • Open the paper cassette slot (located at the bottom of the machine) and confirm paper is loaded.
  • If the cassette is missing, pull it out from the back port, align the paper so the roller can feed it in, and reinsert until you hear a click.
ℹ️

The machine prints layer by layer using thermal dye-sublimation — yellow first, then magenta, then cyan, then lamination. The paper moves in and out during this process. Do not pull it out.

You need the Liene Photo App to design and send your sticker to the machine.

  • Search "Liene Photo" in the App Store (iOS) or Google Play (Android).
  • The app is free to download.
  • If your device is not compatible, ask a Tech Tutor for assistance — they may be able to help you use an alternative device.
⚠️

When prompted, allow Bluetooth and Location permissions. Both are required for stable pairing with the machine. The app will not connect without them.

The app offers several creative options — pick the one that fits your idea:

🗂 Use a Template

Browse 10,000+ free built-in templates across themes, holidays, and styles. Customise text and colours directly in the app.

🖼 Upload Your Own Image

Import a PNG, JPG, or HEIC file from your camera roll. For best results, use a PNG with a transparent background so the AI cutter follows your exact shape.

✏️ Design from Scratch

Use the in-app design tools or third-party apps like Canva, then export and import the image. The AI will auto-detect edges for cutting.

🤖 AI Lab

Upload a photo and pick an AI style — anime, fantasy hero, holiday illustration — and print the result as a unique sticker.

ℹ️

For non-transparent images, use the AI Matting tool inside the app to remove the background automatically before printing.

  1. Press and hold the power button for 3 seconds. The white LED ring will switch to a blinking blue light — this means it's ready to pair.
  2. Make sure Bluetooth is enabled on your phone.
  3. Inside the Liene Photo App, follow the setup prompts and tap Search for Device.
  4. The machine will appear in the list as "PixCut S1" — tap it to pair.
  5. Once connected, the blue light will stop blinking and stay solid.
⚠️

The machine can only be paired to one device at a time. If someone else is connected, the machine won't appear in your list. Ask them to disconnect first, or wait your turn.

Before printing, double-check a few things in the app:

  • Make sure your canvas size matches the paper (4×6" or 4×7"). Mismatched dimensions are the most common cause of paper jams.
  • Use the Preview option to see how cut lines will appear around your sticker.
  • Tap Print and confirm the connection to the PixCut S1.
ℹ️

The machine features automatic paper feeding — once you send the print job, it will handle everything. No need to manually feed the paper.

The PixCut S1 prints in four separate passes:

🟡 Layer 1 — Yellow
🔴 Layer 2 — Magenta
🔵 Layer 3 — Cyan
🏳 Layer 4 — Lamination (protective coat)

You will see the paper slide in and out between each layer — this is completely normal. After printing, the machine uses a 0.1mm blade to kiss-cut your design (cuts the sticker layer, not the backing).

🚫

Do NOT pull the paper out while it is still printing. Do NOT close or exit the app. Wait for the app to show a "Print Completed" notification before removing your sticker.

Once the app displays "Print Completed":

  • The paper will have fully exited the machine — gently take it.
  • Your sticker is already cut — simply peel it from the backing sheet.
  • The sticker is waterproof, fade-resistant, and scratch-resistant thanks to the lamination layer.
  • If you printed multiple stickers on one sheet, remove the completed sheet before starting a new print to avoid cutting errors.
✅

That's it! Enjoy your custom sticker. If you encounter any issues, see the FAQ below or find a Tech Tutor for help.

Troubleshooting & FAQ

Tap a question to reveal the answer.

The machine won't connect / doesn't appear in Bluetooth
  • Make sure the power button was held for 3 full seconds until the LED blinks blue.
  • Check that Bluetooth is enabled on your phone and that the Liene app has Bluetooth and Location permissions.
  • The machine can only connect to one device at a time. If another student is connected, wait for them to disconnect.
  • Try turning the machine off and on again, then search for it fresh in the app.
The paper got jammed inside the machine
  • This is usually caused by using the wrong canvas size in the app. Always set your design to 4×6" or 4×7" — not a custom size.
  • Do not try to force the paper out. Power off the machine and contact a Tech Tutor.
  • Ensure paper is loaded straight and flat in the cassette before printing.
The app crashed or froze during printing
  • This can happen with high memory usage. Close other apps running in the background.
  • Force-quit the Liene app and reopen it — it will attempt to reconnect to the machine.
  • If the paper is mid-print, do not remove it. Wait for the machine to finish its current layer or stop on its own.
  • Contact a Tech Tutor if the issue persists.
My sticker cut didn't follow the right shape
  • For best cutting results, upload a PNG file with a transparent background. The AI cutter traces the non-transparent pixels exactly.
  • If your image has a solid background, use the AI Matting tool in the app to remove it first.
  • Use the Preview button before sending to print to see exactly where cut lines will fall.
Can I use this machine with my laptop or computer?

Currently, the PixCut S1 is mobile-only. It is compatible with iOS and Android phones and tablets. A USB-C port is present on the machine for future PC support, but desktop software is not yet fully functional. Stick to the mobile app for now.

What paper can I use? Can I use any brand?

The machine is designed to work exclusively with Liene-brand 4×6" or 4×7" PET paper. Using third-party paper may result in poor print quality, paper jams, or machine damage. Paper is provided — check the supply next to the machine, or ask a Tech Tutor to restock.

The colors look faded or banded — is something wrong?
  • Some light banding is normal with dye-sublimation printing, especially on large flat-color areas.
  • Make sure the ink cartridge is properly seated. The cartridge slot is on the side of the machine.
  • If the ink is depleted, the cartridge needs replacement — contact a Tech Tutor.
Are the stickers actually waterproof and durable?

Yes! The four-layer thermal dye-sublimation process includes an automatic lamination coat, making stickers waterproof, fade-resistant, and scratch-resistant. They work best on smooth surfaces. They may peel more easily from textured or plastic surfaces.
